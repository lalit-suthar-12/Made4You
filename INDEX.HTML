798 Doctors died during second wave of Covid-19 across country:IMA

Indian Medical Association said that 798 doctors died during the second wave of Covid-19 across the country of which maximum 128 doctors lost their lives in Delhi, followed by Bihar at 115. As per the official note by the IMA, the number of doctors who lost their lives in the pandemic in Delhi stands at 128, followed by Bihar at 115 and Uttar Pradesh at 79. States like Maharashtra and Kerala where the prevalence of the Delta Plus variant of COVID-19 is on a rise also reported deaths of doctors in double digits. Maharashtra reported 23 doctor deaths and Kerala reported 24 deaths. Pondicherry reported the lowest deaths of doctors by logging one death only.

Like every year, the country celebrated National Doctors' Day on July 1 to honour the contributions of the doctors. The first National Doctor's day was celebrated in the country in July 1991.

"PM Modi ensured that doctors should be respected, protected and safeguarded. He also emphasised the need for vaccination. We're celebrating this year's National Doctors' Day (July 1) in remembrance of the veteran Dr BC Roy. We're so much pleased that PM Modi greeted the medical fraternity for the Doctors' Day," the IMA President had said.

About Corona Warriors

MBBS Doctors , MBBS Students , Nurses , Hospitals , Railway Hospitals , LAB Volunteers , Servicemen , NYKS , NSS , NCC NGOS , Anganwadi , Polices , Delivery Agents , Fire Services etc.

Through the Covid-19 pandemic, we have seen examples of exemplary courage among our frontline workers. Now, it’s time to bring their stories of bravery and determination to a bigger audience, so they may become symbols of our collective spirit during a tough time for the country.

During the pandemic, we all are able to live safely from Covid-19 only due to Covid warriors. These warriors are risking their own life to protect the public and to keep us safe. But some irresponsible people are not following all Covid protocols and risking their own lives. I advise everyone to stay at home and follow the rules released by our government to keep us safe.

We should only go out if it is an emergency and wear a double-layered mask. We must follow social distancing and sanitise our hands regularly. Doctors, nurses, other medical staff as well as policemen are working day and night to keep us safe. Even if we thank them as much as we can, is will still be less for the work they are doing for us.

We should obey the rules the policemen ask us to follow. Because of their duty towards public service, they aren’t able to give time to their family. They have to stay away from their family the whole day just to protect the public from the virus.

We can stay away from corona patients, but doctors have to be around the patients to cure them by wearing PPE kits, which make them feel hot and sweaty, while we sit under the fan all day. So, I would again like to thank all our Corona warriors. We should respect them as they are working day and night only for us, keeping our safety above their own family’s.
